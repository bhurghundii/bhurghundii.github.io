I want to start this article off by saying that in no way do I blame my parents for this. I genuinely don't think anyone could have prepared themselves for the world we were getting ourselves into.

# My Dad has a Fun Story He Likes to Tell People at the Dinner Table

My dad once bought a new car, and it took me months to notice. He recalls that I asked him one day if we got a new car, months after we bought the car. The joke is: Vikram gets lost in his own world. I think the story is funny, but it didn't occur to me that at the time, I was going through some serious Internet addiction problems.

Another example of this was when my friends and I sat around looking at old school photos and memories. Memories that I don't have - everything is super fuzzy. I can't remember who my teachers were, which classes I took, what I was into. I do, however, have my old Google Photos storage from back then, and it's just chock-filled with memes, screenshots, and other useless things. I estimate I clocked around 14 hours a day online (including gaming and browsing), and that's when I was not sleeping. Looking at my stats, I had a lot of days where I spent 7 hours just on Counter-Strike. Honestly, it's terrifying. It's like 10 years of my life were missing.

I must've been doing OK because no one seemed to notice it or called me out on it. My grades were alright, and I got into a good university, so not too bad for an autopilot. I reckon if I absolutely bottomed up, I would've had restrictions placed on me.

# My Experience

## What Enabled This?

Luckily for me, I have my old Google Drive account and my Steam account, which has all sorts of useful stuff for this investigation. I don't really have screen time metrics because those weren't tracked, but I do have Google Photos with plenty of screenshots with time at the top of them and Steam times. Looking back at the data, I estimate I was spending 11 hours a day on screens. I estimate it started when I got my first smartphone: Nexus 5. The fact that I could now feed my addiction wherever just shot me. I want to say I felt safe on the internet, but I don't even remember if that was the reason I was going on there.

## Did I Mature or Did I Snap Out of It?

I don't want to attribute this experience to me being a teenager because I genuinely don't remember so much. I don't remember my birthdays, who I hung out with, people during this time period. It's all just a massive blur of video games, programming, and forum browsing. And I am willing to bet other people have gone through it as well.

## Talking About You

This is my experience. It's just a big blur of digital experiences, but I was still talking to people. There were real people interacting on the internet with me. I have no doubts maybe some of them are on internet-induced autopilot just like I was.

I implore you to look at the way you consume your technology and whether you want to be doing that.
