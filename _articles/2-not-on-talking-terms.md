# English

English is a [big deal](https://www.simonandsimon.co.uk/blog/17-english-language-learning-statistics). One might say that it is the default [lingua franca](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/culture-online/case-studies/2022/mar/english-often-considered-de-facto-global-language) and that's partly due to the British Empire doing what it did but also, I presume, because it's a really [easy language to learn and speak](https://christopherwink.com/2018/11/05/english-easy-learn-john-mcwhorter-history-magnificent-bastard-tongue/)

The proliferation of English through social media has broken through to any part of the world that has access to the Internet. Even in India, you can speak a dialect of English (could be [broken English](https://www.npr.org/sections/codeswitch/2014/07/31/336380977/breaking-out-the-broken-english) or a dialect?!) with pretty much anyone including kids. 

# Language in a Post-Brexit World

The UK attitudes towards Europe probably has [some influence](https://www.theguardian.com/education/2019/jul/03/brexit-putting-pupils-off-modern-foreign-languages) on whether we are teaching kids Spanish or German in school. 

Language learning is brutally difficult. It can be difficult to express yourself in your first language but a second one? [Ouch](https://sshjournal.com/index.php/sshj/article/view/1117). 

As someone who's second language is Hindi, surrounded by people who speak it as a First language, it can be uncomfortable to speak non-everyday things with others because of the potential for ridicule and also because [no one wants to speak Hindi with me](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sMac1nxD464&ab_channel=IgneousAbercromby)

I'd imagine that given the exam pressure and grades on the line, this is just amplified. 

# Are we doing enough?

Language teaching in schools (England) - House of Commons Library
In 73 schools, inspectors found that pupils spent [less than an hour a week on their foreign language learning](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/5a80c8ade5274a2e8ab52254/Foreign_languages_and_science_in_primary_schools.pdf). Pupils spent between one and two hours a week learning languages in another 29 schools.

Modern language teacher trainee recruitment for secondary schools as measured by the 2023/24 Initial Teacher Training (ITT) census was below the average for secondary school teachers.

The answer is **no**

# Why the British government needs to care  
Language is how people communicate. Exposure to new cultures will enable better trade deals.

Also, we get a [better reputation abroad.](https://www.reddit.com/r/okmatewanker/comments/14rdal4/free_spanish_lessons_with_barry_63/)

# Why schools need to care  
Learning a different language activates different parts of the brain and trains them. This actually will help students succeed in other areas of their school
