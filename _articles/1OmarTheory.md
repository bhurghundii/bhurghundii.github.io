I would like to introduce you  to Omar. 
Omar is Data Scientist in Vodafone. He lives in Cairo with his family, he supports Real Madrid and thinks that Batman is mankind's greatest achievement.

Omar is also the hardest working engineer I know. The man puts in an incredible amount of work at his day job, then goes home and studies for his MIT Micro-Masters. He has got what it takes to excel in his field, and he is doing it right. 

I have never met Omar. I've only called him once and yet, we have worked together to [model cancer](https://github.com/bhurghundii/ORLIAn), looked after each other in our respective careers and urged each other together to take the better paths when available. 

I wouldn't have met Omar without social media. He first reached out to me on some random Discord server about machine learning, wanting to join up on a project. We've been friends ever since. 

# The evils of social media 

There is a lot of focus currently on how bad social media is. It's a time sink, a cesspit of misinformation and any number of other things. I don't disagree with any of that. I think social media platforms have a long way to in safeguarding their own apps but seldom do we talk about the good that comes out of it. 

I have met some of the most interesting people on social media. I have learned a lot from them, and I have been able to help them in return. I have been able to find people who are interested in the same things as me, and I have been able to find people who are interested in things I know nothing about. 

In the past, the only way you could've made such connections is by traveling to foreign lands or vice versa but now, I am introduced to an entirely different world. I get some (not a great lot admittedly) of insight into how people in other countries think, what they are interested in, and what they are working on, as well as their dreams and struggles. 

# The Omar Theory

The theory goes like this: social media's best use case is to connect people who are passionate about the same things. If you can make at least one friend, you've won. 

# My thoughts on banning social media 

There is a lot of talk in the EU (where I live) about banning social media for public safety. While their intentions are noble in protecting children, you also cut off a key way for people to connect with others. Having a friend who is passionate about the same things as you are is a rare thing, even if you have never met them before.

I will go onto even say that social media companies should help their users develop better deeper connections. It's that connection that makes social media worth it and arguably, more addictive than the dopamine hits of likes and re-tweets or memes. 

And for that, I raise my glass to our digital overlords.
