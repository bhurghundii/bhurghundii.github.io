Decision fatigue is defined as the "deteriorating quality of decisions made by an individual after a long session of decision making" (from Wikipedia)

# Too many Options, Not Enough Time

Making decisions is hard. It's even harder when you have to make a lot of them. I have been working on a project that has a lot of moving parts and I found myself getting bogged down by the sheer number of things I had to do. I would spend hours just trying to figure out what to do next. I was getting nowhere fast.

# Pick One At Random

![homepage](/images/homepage.png)

I built www.pickoneatrandom.com exactly for this purpose. I hope it helps you as much as it has helped me to get into the groove. 

1. Hit "Add a new deck" and enter a name for the deck
2. Hit "Add a new item" and enter a name for the item
3. Add as many items as you want
4. Go back to the main screen and voila! You get one thing random,

Note: it only gives you the random things ONCE per day. This means you can't just keep hitting the button until you get what you want.

## I am lazy 

There are pre-defined decks that you can use if you are feeling lazy. I have a few that I use for my own personal use and I hope you find them useful too.

# How it works

It was very important to me that the app be as simple as possible. I wanted my items to persist so I only had to do the work once and not have touch things again.
With that in mind, I decided to forgo any real backend and just have the app store the data locally.
Turns out this was a great idea because I was able to get the app up and running in a few hours and it works great!
