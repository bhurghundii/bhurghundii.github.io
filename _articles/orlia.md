So, my Christmas holidays didn't happen. Shame. I was going to spend my Christmas holidays alone with my dog [1]. The funny thing about having a dog is they require constant supervision, attention and treats otherwise they get cranky. So I was going to spend a lot of time on my own for about 2 weeks. There was an opportunity to do a whole bunch of stuff I have been holding off. This article is about learning something I've been putting off since I was 8: how to read and write Hindi. 

# What are my goals here?

I am conversational - I can communicate in Hindi but I am limited to everyday conversations with limited feedback (usually because I get an answer instead of being told how badly I butchered a sentence.) This was a benefit because I have a working model I can improve upon so my focus should be on improving those basics. The problem however was I didn't know where I could improve on in terms of my communication. 

In short, my goal is to get an idea of the basics and language features so with further research, I can do better. 

In order to do that, I would need something that gave me a comprehensive overview of the language as opposed to something which just gave me words. A traditional approach, so to speak. 

After checking with Reddit, I got a book that was highly recommended : [Teach Yourself Hindi by Rupert Snell](https://www.amazon.co.uk/Teach-Yourself-Hindi-Complete-Courses/dp/034086687X)

# The plan 

The book goes over a lot of different language functions from oblique case to formalities to tenses. Perfect. I would work through the book and it's exercises to get that understanding. 

There were a lot of new words at the end of each chapter. With things like new words, Anki has always been my go to for remembering any facts. 

# Execution 

Given my family and most of my friends were busy doing fun stuff, I figured I could just turn off my phone and get it done right there and then. This was honestly probably the biggest reason why I learned any Hindi at all and TBH, an approach I am going to take in the future for pretty much anything that needs to get done.

Who would've thought all I need to do to do things is just sit and do nothing but the thing.
