This post is dedicated to my friend, Josh. Josh let me know kindly he really admires the way I think and approach things. 

It's a bit weird because I am working really hard to let go of things, even the way I think and eventually, myself. 

You either do it or don't. There is no Agile, there is no process, there is no framework to getting anything done. There is no 3 step plan and you will be coming up with tons of creative ideas - it's just surrender to the moment. All this stuff is just a way to get you to do it and to put some predictability around doing things. I think its cause its human nature to be afraid of th future so we ideally try to predict it.

## Stay in your lane vs It isn't about you

There is an element of deprogramming to all of this. We spend a lot of our life in some sensible way of living: you go to school, get a job, find a partner, have kids, retire, die. A lot of the risk is removed - there are safe pockets to express yourself and still be productive. It's a path that one can be truly happy with IMO - but I believe it is something you very much have to buy into. You have to become a part of that system. In a way, it doesn't become about you. You **stay in your lane**

I am fortunate (?) enough to have enough shocks to my system to start asking why. I have found when you try to get to the roots of things, there are no real answers in any of this. I don't really think I can live without having those questions answered. 

This is an novel feeling - I feel it comes from a result of realizing that it is not about you. If you decide to stray from your lane, you will find that it's actually OK. The world doesn't care (it was never about you.) Lately, I have been posting more awful unbaked awful (x2) ideas and you know what, nothing other than it being done happens. 

I wish to continue to explore this opportunity a bit more.
